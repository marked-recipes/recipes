## Ingredients

### For the Dough
- [ ] 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- [ ] 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
- [ ] 2 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] 2 teaspoons salt
- [ ] 1 teaspoon sugar
- [ ] 1 1/4 cups warm water (110°F)

### For the Topping
- [ ] 1 can (14 oz) crushed San Marzano tomatoes
- [ ] 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
- [ ] Fresh basil leaves
- [ ] 2 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- [ ] Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

### Prepare the Dough
1. [ ] In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let stand for 5 minutes until foamy
2. [ ] In a large bowl, combine flour and salt
3. [ ] Make a well in the center and add yeast mixture and olive oil
4. [ ] Mix until a dough forms
5. [ ] Knead on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic
6. [ ] Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 1 hour until doubled

### Prepare the Sauce
7. [ ] Combine crushed tomatoes, minced garlic, 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper
8. [ ] Set aside to let flavors meld

### Assemble and Bake
9. [ ] Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C) with pizza stone inside
10. [ ] Punch down dough and divide into 2 balls
11. [ ] Roll out each ball into a 12-inch circle
12. [ ] Transfer to parchment paper
13. [ ] Spread sauce evenly, leaving a 1/2-inch border
14. [ ] Arrange mozzarella slices on top
15. [ ] Drizzle with remaining olive oil
16. [ ] Carefully transfer to hot pizza stone
17. [ ] Bake for 10-12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly
18. [ ] Remove from oven and top with fresh basil leaves
19. [ ] Let cool for 2-3 minutes before slicing

## Notes

- For a crispier crust, pre-bake the dough for 3 minutes before adding toppings
- Fresh mozzarella is key - avoid pre-shredded cheese
- Don't overload with toppings or the crust will be soggy
- A pizza stone makes a huge difference in crust quality
